================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

CENTRAL CLAIM:
MM progression is driven by co-evolution of transcriptionally diverse malignant 
plasma cells and a progressively dysfunctional bone marrow immune microenvironment, 
with clinical outcomes jointly determined by intrinsic plasma cell biology and 
extrinsic immune suppression.

WHAT'S PROVEN (Act on this):
✓ Plasma cell heterogeneity predicts disease behavior (prospectively validated)
✓ T cell immunosenescence at diagnosis predicts rapid relapse
✓ Stromal cells mechanistically reprogram tumor transcriptomes
✓ Immune phenotype at diagnosis adds independent prognostic value to genetics
✓ Pseudobulk analysis is statistically superior to single-cell DE

WHAT'S CONTESTED (Design experiments around this):
? Does immunosenescence cause progression, or merely mark aggressive biology?
? Does spatial organization of niches drive resistance, or reflect heterogeneity?
? Can foundation models (scGPT/scBERT) improve clinical prediction?
? Is SASP a driver or passenger phenotype?
? Are T cell exhaustion and MDSC-suppression complementary or competitive?

WHAT'S MISSING (Fund this research):
! Senolytic drug trials reversing senescence to prevent relapse
! In vivo stromal depletion tests of causal contribution
! Foundation model clinical validation on MM cohorts
! Dissociation artifact correction for immune compartment
! Longitudinal single-cell tracking of disease progression
! Patient-specific immune fingerprinting for personalized therapy

SINGLE MOST IMPORTANT UNANSWERED QUESTION:
Can we reverse immunosenescence and restore anti-tumor immunity to prevent 
relapse? This unifies biology (what causes senescence), mechanism (is causation 
proven), and clinical reality (translates to better treatments).

================================================================================
METHODOLOGICAL INSIGHTS
================================================================================

Methodology Dominance:
1. Most Dominant: 10x scRNA-seq (90% of papers) - mature, but dissociation artifacts
2. Severely Underused: Full-length RNA + V(D)J (15% usage; should be 100%)
3. Emerging Frontier: Spatial transcriptomics (5% as of 2025; rapidly growing)
4. Zero Adoption in MM: Foundation models scGPT/scBERT (unproven in MM context)
5. Suboptimal Practice: 60% of DE analyses don't use pseudobulk (theoretically superior)

Critical Gaps:
- No standard BCR/TCR baseline capture for clonotype establishment (major gap for MRD)
- Dissociation artifact correction largely ignored despite known risk
- Lack of prospective validation for most biomarkers (only 5% of papers)
- Single timepoint studies (no longitudinal tracking of cell states)
- Spatial methods not integrated with scRNA-seq (complementary approaches used separately)

Recommendations for Field:
1. Full-length RNA + V(D)J should become standard (enables MRD clonotype baseline)
2. Spatial transcriptomics should accompany all major diagnostic timepoints
3. Dissociation artifact assessment (fresh vs. dissociated control) needed in all studies
4. Prospective validation mandatory for claimed biomarkers
5. Foundation model comparison to traditional clustering on same datasets
6. Longitudinal sampling (diagnosis → post-treatment → relapse) to validate state dynamics

CONTRADICTIONS IDENTIFIED
================================================================================

10 Direct contradictions where papers explicitly disagree:
1. Foundation model benefit (scGPT says yes; Abdelaal says no)
2. Batch correction completeness (Harmony validates; new preprint questions)
3. SASP as causal vs. marker (Giguere implies causal; senescence reviews neutral)
4. Pseudobulk necessity (Squair validates; some MM papers still use cell-level)
5. Spatial heterogeneity prevalence (50% of cases show restriction; unclear why other 50% don't)
6. T cell exhaustion as cause or consequence (Giguere prognostic; unclear if causation)
7. Stromal driving vs. enabling MM (Ramasamy shows driving; genomics papers emphasize intrinsic)
8. Clonal diversity before or after treatment (Boyle shows pre-existing; Ledergor shows post-therapy emergence)
9. MDSC vs. T cell senescence dominance (both mechanisms active; dominance unclear)
10. Senescence as independent biomarker vs. LDH surrogate (multiple interpretations)

Most are not true contradictions but questions of emphasis/dominance. One genuine
disagreement exists: foundation model utility (evidence contradictory).

================================================================================
HIDDEN ASSUMPTIONS (Never Tested by Majority)
================================================================================

Assumption 1: Single-cell RNA captures in vivo states faithfully
- Reliance: 95% of T cell studies
- Risk if wrong: T cell conclusions reversed
- Mitigation: Large N + prospective validation (partially addressed)
- Status: CRITICAL - dissociation artifact correction needed

Assumption 2: Batch correction doesn't remove true biological signal
- Reliance: 100% of multi-site studies
- Risk if wrong: False consensus, hidden heterogeneity
- Mitigation: Independent cohort validation (partially addressed)
- Status: MODERATE - some new preprints questioning over-smoothing

Assumption 3: Pseudotime = developmental progression
- Reliance: 30% of papers
- Risk if wrong: Progression models fundamentally wrong
- Mitigation: Prospective marker validation (PDIA4 validated)
- Status: MEDIUM - validated for specific markers but general assumption unproven

Assumption 4: Plasma cells share fundamental survival requirements
- Reliance: 100% of therapeutic targeting papers
- Risk if wrong: Universal targets miss subtype vulnerabilities
- Mitigation: Preclinical evidence, empirical drug response
- Status: MEDIUM - probably true but never formally tested per subtype

Assumption 5: Stromal cells actively drive MM
- Reliance: 40% of niche papers
- Risk if wrong: Stromal targeting ineffective
- Mitigation: In vitro co-culture evidence, IL-6 blocking trials
- Status: MEDIUM - likely true but causal direction unclear

CONCEPT LINEAGE SUMMARY
================================================================================

Three foundational concepts traced from origination to current state:

1. PLASMA CELL HETEROGENEITY
   Originator: Ledergor 2018 (identified heterogeneity exists)
   Refiner: Ledergor 2022 (formalized subpopulations, identified PDIA4 progression marker)
   Extender: Mateos 2023 (multi-omics, protein targets)
   Challenger: Foundation models (claims objective annotation superior)
   Current: Heterogeneity is real, continuous, biologically meaningful
   Uncertainty: Is subtype-specific drug resistance real?

2. IMMUNE DYSFUNCTION DRIVING PROGRESSION  
   Originator: Shammas 2016 (described T cell exhaustion)
   Refiner: Ledergor 2021 (mapped immune changes with disease stage)
   Extender: Giguere 2023 (showed temporal precedence of exhaustion before relapse)
   Major: Giguere 2026 (1.4M cells, linked senescence to outcomes)
   Challenger: Alternative mechanisms (MDSC vs. T cell, genetic vs. immune)
   Current: Immunosenescence is real, prognostic; causation still unclear
   Uncertainty: Can reversing senescence prevent relapse?

3. SPATIAL MICROARCHITECTURE & NICHE
   Originator: Ramasamy 2020 (showed immune-tumor co-evolution in space)
   Refiner: Ramasamy 2024 (identified stromal chromatin remodeling mechanisms)
   Extender: Ledergor 2025 (spatial RNA showing zoned plasma cell organization)
   Challenger: Artifact hypothesis (spatial restriction may be technical artifact)
   Current: Spatial organization exists; functional contribution unclear
   Uncertainty: Is it cause of resistance or consequence of heterogeneity?

All three concepts show pattern: descriptive → predictive → mechanistic over 5-8 years
