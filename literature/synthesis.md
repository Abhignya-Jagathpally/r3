# Multiple Myeloma Single-Cell Genomics: Field Synthesis (400 Words)

## What the Field Collectively Believes

Multiple myeloma is a transcriptionally heterogeneous malignancy where progression is jointly determined by intrinsic plasma cell biology, genetic subclones, and a dysfunctional bone marrow immune microenvironment. Single-cell transcriptomics reveals that malignant plasma cells occupy a continuum of differentiation states within each patient, with stemness and proliferative capacity varying dramatically across subpopulations despite shared genetic origin. The bone marrow niche actively selects for and sustains specific plasma cell states through stromal-mediated chromatin remodeling and sustained expression of growth factors. Concurrently, the immune microenvironment progressively dysfunctions through accumulation of senescent T cells with exhaustion markers (PD-1+, CD28−, CD57+) and activation of myeloid-derived suppressor cells that suppress anti-tumor immunity. Disease progression (MGUS → SMM → MM) involves both genomic alterations (driver mutations accumulating stepwise) and transcriptomic state transitions that reflect increased stemness, enhanced proliferation, and progressive immune escape. These twin mechanisms—intrinsic and microenvironmental—conspire to produce the clinical phenotype of rapidly progressive vs. indolent disease.

## What Remains Contested

The field disagrees on **causality vs. correlation** in three key areas. First, does T cell immunosenescence *drive* rapid progression, or is it a *marker* of underlying aggressive tumor biology? Giguere et al. (2026) argue senescence is prognostic; yet no experiments reverse senescence to improve immunity. Second, which mechanism of immune suppression dominates—T cell intrinsic senescence or MDSC-mediated suppression? Both exist; dominance unclear and likely patient-specific. Third, does plasma cell spatial localization reflect active homing to niches (tumor-cell driven) or passive selection by niche stromal cells? Current evidence suggests bidirectional crosstalk, but causality and controllability remain open. Additionally, foundation models for cell annotation (scGPT, scBERT) promise improved robustness through pre-training, but contradictory evidence (Abdelaal et al.) shows simple baselines match complex models, leaving clinical utility unproven.

## What Is Proven Beyond Reasonable Doubt

(1) Transcriptional heterogeneity exists within clonal plasma cell populations and predicts disease behavior (PDIA4 validated prospectively). (2) Immune exhaustion markers at diagnosis predict relapse (temporal precedence shown). (3) Stromal cells mechanically reprogram tumor cell chromatin. (4) Spatial heterogeneity exists in bone marrow architecture with zone-specific immune/stromal composition. (5) Disease progression involves both genetic events and transcriptional state transitions. (6) Pseudobulk analysis is statistically superior to single-cell DE for disease stage comparisons.

## The Single Most Important Unanswered Question

**Can we reverse immunosenescence and restore anti-tumor immunity to prevent relapse?** This question unifies biology (what causes senescence?), mechanism (is senescence causal?), and clinical reality (patients with senescent T cells at diagnosis progress rapidly). If senescence is reversible through senolytic drugs, microenvironmental modification, or checkpoint blockade, immunosenescence becomes actionable; if irreversible, it remains a prognostic marker only. Answering this determines whether the future of MM immunotherapy focuses on eliminating senescent cells or replacing them with engineered immunity.
